We Kept on Keepin' On

Music, Memory, and a Reunion with Ray White

Music, memory, and social critique — a collaboration rooted in 1960s San Francisco, rekindled fifty years later.

The Story

The Videos

The more things change…it's 2026, not 1969…the more they stay the same. Fifty years since the days we played in a San Francisco band called The Brothers And, here in Japan, I discovered Ray White's fine album, Wrinkle In The Plan. Back in 1971, our fiery song of political change, Ain't Gonna Kill the Spirit, had been a near-miss hit, but it never left my heart. Connecting with Ray motivated me to rework and videoized that song for the Age of Trump – you can check it out below.

But hearing a post-Zappa Ray White, his unfettered, authentic personality in new originals touched me deeply, and it motivated me to create other videos to bring Ray's songs to life. After chatting with him online, I added horn parts and sax and flute solos to flesh out his tracks. Please check them out too – including the beautiful new bonus track, "Angels." The true voice of Ray Shirley White is a bright light in these troubled times.

About

Arnold Baruch

Arnold Baruch is a musician, educator, and multimedia artist based in Tokyo, where he has lived since 1992. He teaches at a Japanese university, composes and records original music, and produces video work that blends musical performance with visual storytelling.

In the late 1960s and early 70s, Arnold played in The Brothers And, a San Francisco band that included a young Ray White. Their song Ain't Gonna Kill the Spirit earned national airplay and came close to breaking through. The music never left him.

Over fifty years later — and across the Pacific — he found Ray again.

Artist

Ray White

Ray Shirley White is a singer and guitarist whose voice became central to some of the most adventurous music of the 1970s and 80s. Born in the American south, his journey took him through the heart of America and its music before landing him on stage with Frank Zappa, where he became a featured performer across some of Zappa's most celebrated touring years.

But Ray has always been someone else: a thoughtful songwriter with a unique voice of his own. The songs on his album Wrinkle In The Plan — soulful, warm, politically awake, and deeply personal — represent an artist who never stopped growing. These videos exist to bring those songs the visual life they deserve.
