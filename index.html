The Story

Fifty years.
One open door.

I first met Ray White in 1969, when we were both playing in a band called The Brothers And. We spent about three years making music together — music that eventually led to the release of Ray's song, Ain't Gonna Kill the Spirit, with real national airplay and a near-miss at history.

Ray's path took him to a much bigger stage as a featured performer with Frank Zappa. My own road took me to Japan in 1992. Then, in 2023, I discovered Ray's album Wrinkle In The Plan. Hearing these new originals after all those years had a powerful effect on me.

It reminded me of the artist — and the friend — I knew back in the day.

"After all these years, Ray's music still carries the soulful spirit it always did. For me, hearing him again felt a little like coming home."

— Arnold Baruch, Tokyo

Ray White

Singer · Guitarist · Songwriter

Ray White is a singer and guitarist whose voice became central to some of the most adventurous music of the 1970s and 80s. Mississippi born, Chicago raised, Bay Area forged — his journey took him through the heart of American music before landing him on stage with Frank Zappa, where he became a featured performer across some of Zappa's most celebrated touring years.

But Ray has always been someone else: a thoughtful songwriter with a unique voice of his own. The songs on his album Wrinkle In The Plan — soulful, warm, politically awake, and deeply personal — represent an artist who never stopped growing.

These videos exist to bring those songs the visual life they deserve.
